Physics Calendar

A list of active major physics projects around the world

Home

There are many physics collaborations all around the world currently conducting some incredibly interesting and inspiring research! As a couple of passionate physics graduate students, we decided to make this website to have an easily accessible list of every planned milestone for some of these projects!

We're still under construction, so not everything us up here yet!

Schedule

Project Description Next Big Event Date
Event Horizon Telescope The Event Horizon Telescope is an international collaboration aiming to capture the first image of a black hole by creating a virtual Earth-sized telescope first results soon!
LIGO The Laser Interferometer Gravitational-wave Observatory (LIGO). (Add more info here) 3rd run early 2019
LHC The Large Hadron Collider (ATLAS, CMS, etc) what's next? who knows
JWST The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the premier next-gen space observatory, with a 6.5m infrared telescope. Launch 2021
LSST The Large Synoptic Survey Telescope in Chile will compile the largest public data set in the world. Probing the nature of dark matter, dark enery, and more. Full science operations 2023
LISA The Laser Interferometer Space Antenna (LISA) is a space-based observatory composed of three spacecraft in an equilateral triangle 1 million miles on each side. It will be able to detect gravitational waves of longer wavelength than LIGO, probing super-massive black hole mergers and extreme mass ratio inspirals. Launch approx 2034
